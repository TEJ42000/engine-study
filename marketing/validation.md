## 1. Who it's for

Law students (initially RUG LLB International/European & Dutch Law) who want to
improve exam *technique*, not just accumulate notes. No timing games — this is for
anyone preparing for or retaking a law exam, whenever they choose to engage.

## 2. The honest angle

Law exams reward execution, not recognition. Flashcards and summaries test whether
you *recognise* material; exams test whether you can *reproduce and apply* it cold,
in the right order, under time. Engine Study drills the reproduce-and-apply part and
shows you exactly where you drop marks. (This is a plain description of how the app
works — no embellishment needed.)

## 3. What v1 actually does — advertise ONLY these

- **Cold-recall drills.** Type your procedure from memory first; only then does the
  app reveal the model answer for you to self-check. Content stays hidden until you
  commit a written attempt.
- **Failure-taxonomy diagnostics.** Every miss is logged as `GATE_SKIP`,
  `WRONG_TOOL`, or `PRECISION`. Over a block, your per-course leak profile shows your
  dominant failure pattern — not "you're weak on European Law," but "you skip
  threshold gates" or "you fumble exact citations."
- **Precision checks.** Drill just the exact article numbers / case names /
  qualifiers, separately from full-sequence recall.
- **Two-axis maturity.** See the engines you *understand* but can't yet retrieve
  cold under fatigue — the state that actually costs marks.
- **Mock diagnostics.** Record your own past-paper results, tag each miss to an
  engine and a failure type, and get a focused drill list.
- **Your data is yours.** One-click export/import; everything stays local.

## 4. On the roadmap — clearly labelled, NOT available yet

Say "planned" every time. Never imply these exist.
