## PART A — ENGINES

**Engine 1 — Tripartite Framework (liability spine)**
- Gate: Each stage is a gate. Stage 1 must be *fully* satisfied before Stage 2 is touched; if any stage fails → stop, no liability ("scream it in the middle of the night").
- Steps: (1) Legal Elements — actus reus + mens rea (+ causation for result crimes); (2) Unlawfulness — check justifications; (3) Culpability — check excuses.
- Trigger: any "is X criminally liable?" Part A problem. Not triggered for pure theory questions; narrowed to Stage 1 only when the question asks *solely* about intent.
- Satellites: mens rea taxonomy (intent / dolus eventualis / conscious negligence, distinguished by the Frank-Formula hypothetical); self-defence Art. 41(1) (7 criteria) + self-defence excess Art. 41(2); necessity/duress Art. 40 *overmacht* (operates at Stage 2 *or* Stage 3); participation Arts. 47–51; inchoate offences (attempt, Art. 45); legality principle (lex praevia / lex mitior exception) as the foundational threshold.
- Maturity reached: advanced — drilled to recall level, converted into MCQ banks + model answers, applied to practice scenarios; internalised the three golden rules (stop-when-a-stage-fails, justifications ≠ excuses, intent ≠ guilt).

**Engine 2 — Causation (Stage-1 result-attribution sub-engine)**
- Gate: Factual causation must be established first; only then does legal causation open.
- Steps: (1) Factual — but-for / conditio sine qua non (simple test); (2) Legal — weigh the ~8 legal-causation concepts (culpability of perpetrator, probability of the result, intervening causes, etc.) to decide *reasonable attribution*.
- Trigger: result crimes (homicide, GBH) where the conduct→result link is contestable — especially intervening acts.
- Satellites: novus actus / intervening causes, foreseeability & probability, victim's own conduct.
- Maturity reached: solid/working — dedicated WG notes produced; clear on "factual = mechanical test, legal = weighing exercise."

**Engine 3 — IRAC (answer-structure wrapper, both parts)**
- Gate: Identify the *precise* Issue the question asks — answer that, and only that (scope discipline).
- Steps: Issue → Rule → Application/Analysis → Conclusion.
- Trigger: every case-based/application question (Part A and Part B); exam signals it for scenarios, not pure theory.
- Satellites: the substantive engines it wraps — Tripartite (Part A), Convention-analysis (Part B); the observation that the ECtHR itself reasons in IRAC.
- Maturity reached: advanced — used as the universal wrapper across all model answers.

**Engine 4 — ECHR Convention-Analysis (Part B spine)**
- Gate: Applicability first — is the article even engaged? Autonomous interpretation decides (e.g. Engel/Öztürk criteria for whether a matter is "criminal"; absolute vs qualified right).
- Steps: (1) Applicability (autonomous concepts); (2) Interference/violation on the article's specific test; (3) For qualified rights (Art. 5, 8) — justification: lawful + legitimate aim + necessary/proportionate; (4) Art. 6 — "proceedings as a whole" overall-fairness assessment.
- Trigger: Part B human-rights problems under Art. 3, 5, 6, 8.
- Satellites (case-tests): Öztürk/Engel (autonomous "criminal charge"); Vendittelli (4-factor); Brogan (4-day rule); Letellier (ongoing grounds for detention); Salduz (access to a lawyer); Jalloh (Art. 3-tainted evidence pierces the *Saunders* will-independent exception); Gäfgen (causal-chain / break); Khan (Art. 8 breach ≠ automatic Art. 6 breach); De Cubber (subjective + objective impartiality); Schatschaschwili (3-step absent-witness test); Colozza & Lala (trial in absentia / right to be present and defended); Niemietz (Art. 8 covers professional premises).
- Maturity reached: advanced — 60-question dedicated case-law quiz across all 13 cases; drilled facts/rules/outcomes; internalised the nuanced distinctions (Jalloh exception, Khan principle).

## PART B — MECHANICS

**Recurring error types (GATE_SKIP / WRONG_TOOL / PRECISION)**
- PRECISION — the dominant, genuinely recurring type. Oversimplifications caught and corrected: e.g. necessity (Art. 40) stated as *only* a Stage-2 justification when *overmacht* spans Stage 2 **and** Stage 3; plus source-fidelity corrections where content had to be grounded in Keiler & Roef / the actual judgment rather than plausible-sounding filler.
- WRONG_TOOL — attested, less frequent. Reaching for the wrong doctrine/article: conflating Art. 40 (necessity/*overmacht*, threat from circumstances) with Art. 41 (self-defence/*noodweer*, threat from a human attack); and misconceiving the tripartite framework as a test for "is this criminal?" rather than a structured *liability* analysis.
- GATE_SKIP — mostly guarded-against, not a recurring *committed* error. It shows up in our work as the discipline we repeatedly drilled ("never analyse Stage 2 if Stage 1 fails; don't raise justifications before elements are met"), i.e. a risk the framework is built to prevent, rather than a mistake we kept having to undo.

**What fixed each error**
- PRECISION → re-anchor to the primary source (textbook / lecture notes / judgment text) and tighten the statement; corrections incorporated directly when challenged.
- WRONG_TOOL → clean side-by-side distinction (threat source / stage / legal test) and re-statement of the framework's actual purpose.
- GATE_SKIP → sequential-gate discipline baked into every model answer, with an explicit "STOP" instruction at each stage.

**The diagnosis loop**
Challenge or question posed → search project knowledge / primary sources *before* answering (never from vibes) → answer built in IRAC + the relevant engine → Matej stress-tests it with a conceptual follow-up → correction incorporated directly → distilled into a revision artefact (PDF / quiz / model answer). In short: challenge → source-check → structure → verify → bank it.

**What was distinct about THIS course**
Two parallel legal systems assessed in a single closed-book essay exam (40 pts, pass 22): Part A civil-law substantive doctrine (Dutch Criminal Code with German comparative concepts) versus Part B ECHR/ECtHR case law — two different engines, one IRAC wrapper. Comparative (Dutch + German) rather than single-jurisdiction, and unusually reliant on reading the actual ECtHR judgments, with zero tolerance for fabrication.

**Highest-value move**
Converting every topic into retrieval-practice tools (MCQ banks, flashcards, model answers) plus IRAC-structured worked answers grounded strictly in primary sources — and letting Matej's conceptual challenges drive the corrections — instead of passive re-reading.
