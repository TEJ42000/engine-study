## PART A — ENGINES

### Engine: E1 — Treaty-provision direct effect
- **Gate:** provision must be clear/precise, unconditional, not dependent on further implementation; **and** direct effect must be established *before* supremacy is invoked (the Colombi Ciacchi Week 1 warning).
- **Steps:** (1) Issue → (2) Rule: *Van Gend en Loos* (26/62), vertical → (3) is it horizontal? *Defrenne II* → (4) apply to the exact wording of the Treaty article → (5) Conclusion.
- **Trigger:** an individual invokes a Treaty article (e.g. Art. 30 / Art. 157 TFEU); check public-vs-private party.
- **Satellites:** *Defrenne II* (horizontal DE of Treaty provisions); *Costa v ENEL* / *Simmenthal II* downstream.
- **Maturity reached:** stable — complete standalone decision tree (vertical VGL + horizontal Defrenne II).

### Engine: E2 — Regulation direct effect
- **Gate:** Regulations are directly applicable (Art. 288(2) TFEU); reproducing them in national law is impermissible.
- **Steps:** (1) *Leonesio* (Regulations create invokable individual rights) → (2) *Simmenthal II* (national court disapplies conflicting national law) → (3) apply.
- **Trigger:** instrument is a Regulation.
- **Satellites:** *Simmenthal II*, *Fratelli Variola*.
- **Maturity reached:** drafted (partial) — horizontal branch was cut off mid-build in the March decision-tree session; later folded into the master revision file (final-state completeness: unknown).

### Engine: E3 — Directive effects sequence (indirect → direct → state liability)
- **Gate:** *Marshall* bar — no horizontal direct effect of Directives (Art. 288(3) TFEU) → **indirect effect must be assessed first**.
- **Steps:** (1) Indirect effect (*Faccini Dori* para. 26; *Dominguez* para. 23; contra legem limit) → (2) Direct effect (*Marshall*, vertical only; emanation of state) → (3) State liability (*Francovich*; *Brasserie*: rights + sufficiently serious breach + causal link).
- **Trigger ("Faccini Dori pattern"):** a Directive + non/incorrect transposition + economic loss/sum of money + horizontal dispute.
- **Satellites:** *Adeneler*, *Dansk Industri*, *Egenberger*, *Bauer*, *Francovich*, *Brasserie/Factortame*.
- **Maturity reached:** drafted → stable — narrative-only at the March snapshot (flagged as *the* highest-loss sequencing point, no clean gatekeeper tree), then consolidated in the master file + exam reader.

### Engine: E4 — Supremacy & disapplication
- **Gate:** only triggered when a party argues national law should prevail over EU law; presupposes direct effect already established.
- **Steps:** (1) *Costa v ENEL* (EU law prevails regardless of rank/chronology) → (2) *Solange I* para. 3 (prevails even over national constitutional rights) → (3) *Simmenthal II* (immediate disapplication, no waiting).
- **Trigger:** "our national law is newer / constitutional / better-quality."
- **Satellites:** *Solange I*, *Simmenthal II*, *Internationale Handelsgesellschaft*.
- **Maturity reached:** stable — complete decision tree with scenario→case-matching table.

### Engine: E5 — Charter horizontal effect
- **Gate:** Art. 51 CFR scope gate (Charter binds MS only when implementing EU law) applied **before** any HDE analysis; keep the *Marshall* Directive bar **separate** from the Charter HDE channel.
- **Steps:** (1) Art. 51 gate → (2) indirect effect (*Egenberger* paras 71–72; *Bauer* paras 65–68; contra legem) → (3) Charter HDE where the right is sufficient in itself (via Arts 21/31/47).
- **Trigger:** fundamental-rights dimension in a private dispute + a Directive giving expression to a Charter right.
- **Satellites:** *Dominguez*, *Kücükdeveci*, *Mangold*.
- **Maturity reached:** stable (exam-ready) — built in Week 6 material; Charter Arts 21/31/47/51/52 annexed verbatim in the exam reader.

### Engine: E6 — Legal basis / competences
- **Gate:** legal basis set by aim + content; twin-purpose measures resolved by the predominant purpose.
- **Steps:** (1) identify aim + content → (2) centre-of-gravity test (*Tobacco Advertising I*) → (3) single correct legal basis → (4) validity/annulment consequence.
- **Trigger:** dispute over which Treaty article a measure rests on / competence challenge.
- **Satellites:** *Tobacco Advertising I* (C-376/98) — annulled for competence, **not** a subsidiarity/proportionality authority.
- **Maturity reached:** stable — covered in Week 3 competences notes; essay-usable.

### Engine: E7 — State responsibility (ARSIWA)
- **Gate:** establish **breach + attribution before consequences**; Art. 3 ARSIWA / Art. 27 VCLT — domestic law is never a defence.
- **Steps:** (1) wrongful act = breach + attributable conduct → (2) attribution: Art. 4 (organs), 5 (governmental authority), 7 (ultra vires), 8 (effective control — *Nicaragua*; "in respect of each specific operation" — *Genocide Case*), 11 (adoption — *Tehran Hostages*) → (3) circumstances precluding wrongfulness → (4) consequences: cessation + full reparation (Arts 30–37; *Chorzów*).
- **Trigger:** state conduct harming another state/individuals; private actors possibly linked to a state.
- **Satellites:** *Nicaragua v USA* (1986), *Genocide Case* (Bosnia v Serbia, 2007), *Tehran Hostages* (1980).
- **Maturity reached:** stable — complete essay model (attribution + breach).

### Engine: E8 — Human-rights restriction analysis
- **Gate:** limitations before derogations; Art. 4 ICCPR derogation cannot apply before an **official proclamation** of a public emergency — conduct before proclamation is assessed under limitations only (temporal precision).
- **Steps:** (1) identify right → (2) non-derogable? (Art. 4(2): Arts 6, 7, 8(1)–(2), 11, 15, 16, 18) → (3) limitation test (legitimate aim + necessity/proportionality) → (4) derogation only on valid Art. 4 emergency (proclamation + strictly required + notification to UN SG).
- **Trigger:** state restricting an ICCPR/ECHR right; emergency measures.
- **Satellites:** ICCPR limitation clauses Arts 18(3), 19(3), 21, 22.
- **Maturity reached:** stable — doctrine + MCQ model complete; essay-usable.

### Engine: E9 — IL MCQ topic-recognition cluster
- **Gate:** n/a (recognition-level).
- **Steps:** (1) read stem → (2) match fact pattern to correct doctrine → (3) on two plausible options, pick the one with the precisely correct authority; watch "not correct"/"except" phrasing.
- **Trigger:** MCQ stems on sources, monism/dualism, jurisdiction, immunities, statehood, ICJ jurisdiction/dispute settlement.
- **Satellites:** topic-specific (e.g. UDHR non-binding; ECtHR jurisdiction compulsory not separately accepted; Art. 36(2) optional-clause declaration).
- **Maturity reached:** tested — 8 MCQ topic models built and consolidated into the MCQ bank / revision portal (28 questions); reflex-level unconfirmed.

## PART B — MECHANICS

**Recurring error types**

- **GATE_SKIP** — the core doctrinal failure the whole enterprise was built to prevent. *Topics hit:* jumping straight to supremacy/disapplication (*Costa*/*Simmenthal*) without first proving direct effect (E1/E4); running direct effect before indirect effect on Directives (E3); skipping the Art. 51 CFR gate before Charter HDE (E5); applying an Art. 4 ICCPR derogation before an official proclamation (E8).
- **WRONG_TOOL** — build-side, not doctrinal. *Topics hit:* the exam-reader/PDF build — `curia.europa.eu` (redirecting to infocuria) returns `403 host_not_allowed`; `web_fetch` returns re-typeset text that would count as a "modified document" under the annotation rule; ReportLab fixed-width cells / custom flowables → `LayoutError` and overlapping text.
- **PRECISION** — citation-level, both strands. *Topics hit:* worksheet typo "Simmenthal 107/77" corrected to **106/77**; pinning holdings to exact paragraphs (*Dominguez* 23; *Egenberger* 71–72; *Bauer* 65–68); effective control "each specific operation" (*Genocide Case*); pre-proclamation conduct → limitations only (E8); non-breaking hyphen (U+2011) rendering as tofu in citations.

**What fixed each error**

- **GATE_SKIP** → the fixed ordered analytical sequence (indirect → direct → supremacy → state liability), each doctrine given its own gate and correctly matched authority; the "supremacy presupposes direct effect" rule; sequencing rules memorised as hard order. (Note: our records show these fixes came from building the ordered trees + the person's live corrections of framing — not from a logged rapid-fire routing or interleaving drill.)
- **WRONG_TOOL** → EUR-Lex CELEX URL pattern in place of curia; downloading unmodified official PDF pages (with a `PAGE_OVERRIDES` dict for the five old page-numbered cases); replacing custom flowables / fixed cells with `Paragraph` + `HRFlowable` and nested-`Table` colour boxes.
- **PRECISION** → cross-verifying citations via project-knowledge search *before* writing (lookup practice against the source), plus a unicode-normalization QA pass.

**The diagnosis loop**

Weaknesses surfaced through three actual channels, not a single sat-mock loop: (1) **doctrinal triage** — what instrument? (Treaty / Regulation / Directive / Charter) → who are the parties? (vertical vs horizontal) → is there damage/cost? — this triage selects the engine and its gates, then cites the authority matched to the *specific argument raised*; (2) **live user correction** — framing/content errors were caught and corrected in-conversation (e.g. "Direct Effect is not Week 5"; the 107/77 → 106/77 fix), which re-pointed the build; (3) **build validation** — run → read the proxy `x-deny-reason` / traceback → swap tool or approach → re-validate render. The v2.0 method *prescribed* a cold mock (March 2024 paper, Phase 1) + fresh mock (June 2024 resit, Phase 3) + 24h post-mortem as the intended diagnostic spine; whether those mocks were sat and what they leaked is **unknown** (not logged).

**What was distinct about THIS course**

Two parallel strands under one open-book exam, graded on the **reasoning path, not the conclusion** ("disapply" alone scores zero). The strict annotation rule (highlights / underlining / tabs only, no added or modified content) forced an "exact official pages" fidelity constraint onto the exam reader — which is what generated the WRONG_TOOL build errors. EU doctrine is sequencing-heavy, so marks are lost on *order*, and every rule must be pinned to article + case + paragraph. The method flexed toward heavy front-loaded infrastructure (doctrine cards, ordered gate-trees, a tabbed open-book reader, an MCQ portal) rather than pure memorization drilling — it is an applied, path-graded course.

**Highest-value move**

Building the ordered EU analytical sequence with each doctrine's gate and its *correctly matched* authority — because it directly targets where the exam actually deducts marks (the reasoning path and case-to-argument matching), not the outcome.

## PART C — ONE-LINE SUMMARY

An IRAC-driven, dual-strand exam-engine set where correct *sequencing and pinpoint authority* are the whole game — GATE_SKIP was the enemy, exact-official-source fidelity was the binding constraint, and the ordered EU direct-effect chain with matched authorities was the highest-value build.
